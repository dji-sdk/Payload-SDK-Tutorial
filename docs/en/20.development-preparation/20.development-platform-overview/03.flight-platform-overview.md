The Matrice 400 is DJI's next-generation heavy-lift flight platform designed for industry-grade applications. It features system-level enhancements for complex environmental operations and high-reliability automated missions. The platform delivers multidimensional improvements in environmental awareness, safety redundancy, and image transmission capabilities, meeting the demands of high-requirement scenarios such as inspection, emergency response, security, and automated operations.
This section primarily introduces the key systems and modules within the Matrice 400 flight platform that are closely related to flight perception, safety assurance, and image transmission. The locations of each system module are as follows:

## 1. FPV Camera

The Matrice 400 features a dedicated **FPV camera**, primarily used for real-time video display and situational awareness during flight.
The FPV camera provides forward-facing visual information to the flight controller and operator, enabling pilots to perform precise maneuvers in complex environments. The camera feed displays in real time on terminals like DJI Pilot, supporting takeoff, landing, route navigation, and manual takeover operations in challenging scenarios.

## 2. Omnidirectional Vision System

The Matrice 400 integrates an **omnidirectional vision system** that perceives the surrounding environment through multi-directional visual sensors.
This system provides real-time obstacle detection in multiple directions—front, rear, left, right, up, and down—establishing foundational environmental awareness for the flight platform. Its primary functions include:
- Obstacle detection and avoidance during flight  
- Enhancing flight safety in low-altitude and complex environments
- Supporting stable execution of automated flight missions



## 3. Rotating Lidar

To enhance perception capabilities in complex environments, the Matrice 400 is equipped with **Rotating lidar**.
This sensor uses laser scanning to gather distance information about the surrounding environment. Its sensor direction scans horizontally in front, rear, left, and right, providing stable obstacle detection capabilities in low-light, backlit, or visually challenging scenarios. This module works in tandem with the visual system to significantly improve the flight platform's safety and reliability in complex spaces.


## 4. Top Lidar
The Matrice 400 also incorporates a top lidar for sensing the space above the aircraft.
This top lidar primarily detects overhead obstacles such as bridge structures, indoor ceilings, or other elevated components. During ascent, close-structure flight, or operations in semi-enclosed spaces, it effectively compensates for visual system blind spots, elevating overall flight safety.

## 5. Cellular Dongle Compartment

The Matrice 400 features a dedicated **Cellular DongleCompartment** to expand the platform's video and data transmission capabilities.
This bay supports higher-quality, more stable image and data transmission, adapting to complex electromagnetic environments and long-range operational demands. The module provides reliable transmission assurance for real-time monitoring, remote operation, and data backhaul in industrial applications, serving as a critical component for automated and remote operations.

## 6. 3D Infrared Sensing System

The Matrice 400 integrates a **3D Infrared Sensing System** to enhance the flight platform's perception capabilities in low-visibility environments.
This system detects surrounding obstacles during nighttime, haze, or low-light conditions. It integrates with visual and LiDAR systems through multi-sensor fusion, providing additional flight safety assurance for specialized operations like emergency response and night flights.

## 7. Downward Vision System

The Matrice 400 is equipped with a **Downward Vision System**, primarily designed to perceive the environment beneath the aircraft.
This system plays a crucial role during takeoff, landing, and low-altitude flight phases, assisting the platform in precise hovering, stable landing, and ground environment awareness. In areas with weak GPS signals or indoor environments, the Downward Vision System provides essential positioning and stability support for flight control.
